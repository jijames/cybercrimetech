James tags: Research, Cybercrime, Cyberwarfare, Discussion, Cyber Peacekeeping modified_time: '2017-01-31T18:00:00.628+09:00' --- In 2013, I was the moderator of a panel discussion dealing cyber crime and cyber security at the Seoul Conference on Cyberspace (Youth Forum) 2013. At that panel, I met Nikolay Akatyev. He was an essay finalist with the topic "Proposal of Policy to Create International Cyber Peacekeepers". His proposal was the just start of an idea, but I thought it was very interesting. After that meeting, we discussed the idea further, and eventually decided to expand it. In 2015, we finally published "[Cyber Peacekeeping](http://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-25512-5_10)" at the International Conference on Digital Forensics and Cyber Crime 2015. In that publication we outlined the tasks, scope and organizational framework that could be the basis of Cyber Peacekeeping. We tried to make the content as practical as possible. In later works, we looked at legal requirements for establishing Cyber Peacekeeping, and most recently we finished paper showing that the UN's [Digital Blue Helmets](https://unite.un.org/digitalbluehelmets/) could potentially be a good start to Cyber Peacekeeping, but - for now - does not cover anywhere near the scope required for real peacekeeping efforts in cyberspace.
